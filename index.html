Mountain Biking

History of Mountain Biking

  • Late 1800s: First examples of bikes modified for off road
  • 1900s-1960s: Bicycles are ridden off-road and used as part of Cyclocross to keep fit during the winter
  • 1966: D. Gwynn built a rough terrain bicycle, he named it a "mountain bicycle", possibly the first use of the name
  • 1970s-1980: Several groups of riders from different areas of the USA make valid claims to starting mountain biking as a sport. Manufacturers start producing mountain bikes in the late 70s and early 80s
  • 1990: First mountain bike World Championships
  • 1996: Mountain biking became part of the Olympics at the Atlanta Games
  • 1990s-2000s: Mountain biking starts becoming a more mainstream activity

Different types of Mountain Biking

  • Cross Country Mountain Biking: General off-road biking along trials and fire roads
  • Enduro or All-Mountain Biking: Involves all types of terrain which can include downhill sections, technical descents and switchback climbs
  • Downhill Mountain Biking: Using specific downhill mountain bikes which are much heavier and built for downhill descents
